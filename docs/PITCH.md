# Mallard - the financial advisor for the people advisors ignore.

Real, fiduciary-grade portfolio management given free to anyone, by an AI honest enough to tell you when NOT to invest.

## Why it matters

Good financial advice is still locked behind roughly 1% AUM fees, big account minimums, and institutions that make their money after you invest. Most consumer finance apps do the opposite of fiduciary care: they nudge people to buy more, trade more, and stay engaged.

Mallard gives wealth-manager-grade advice for free, and it can say no. If debt, cash reserves, or basic stability should come first, Mallard shows the math and stops the user before a bad investment decision.

## What makes it different

- The Greenlight responsibility gate can halt you before investing, and it shows the debt and emergency-fund math behind that call.
- An LLM elicits the user's situation and preferences, while a deterministic engine makes the portfolio decisions. No hallucinated numbers.
- Real quant depth: two-axis risk profiling, ERC/Black-Litterman/CVaR optimization, Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage, Monte Carlo projections, 20-year backtests, and tax-aware rebalancing.
- Live Alpaca brokerage execution connects advice to actual implementation.
- It works end-to-end: real FastAPI backend, persistence, passing test suite, and deployable architecture.

## The anti-Robinhood

Others gamify buying and usually frame the answer as "invest." Mallard is honest and on your side. It is designed to protect people from bad timing, bad incentives, and advice that only serves the platform.

## 60-second demo script

1. Land on Mallard: "Fiduciary-grade advice. Free by default."
2. Show a HALT persona. The Greenlight gate explains why debt or an insufficient emergency fund comes first, with the math visible.
3. Switch to a GREENLIGHT persona using DEV: SKIP.
4. Edit the portfolio with the risk dial and sliders, then refresh or navigate to show that the changes persist.
5. Change a preference and watch the engine reweight the portfolio.
6. Ask Mallard a question and show that the assistant explains the decision without inventing numbers.
7. Close: "Greenlight elicits, the engine decides, and it will tell you the truth - even when the truth is not yet."
